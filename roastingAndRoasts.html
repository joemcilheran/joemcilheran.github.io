Roasting and Roasts

 
Roasting coffee, like all cooking is a little bit science, and a little bit feel. 
Different roasting times will yeild different roasts with very different flavor profiles, 
and different caffiene levels. At a certain point in the roast, the bean will reach 
a high enough temperature to audibly crack. This first crack is followed later by a second crack
and both are good indications of what sort of roast you will get. Below is a table of 
roasts with approximate roasting times and flavor profiles.
Roast Name Roasting Time Color Flavor Body Acidity Caffiene
Light Roast Right after first Crack Light Brown Grainy Mild Full Bright High
City Roast 1 Minute after first Crack Medium Brown balanced roast and origin Medium Medium Medium
Vienna Roast During Second Crack Dark Brown Bittersweet roast Light Muted Medium
Italian Roast After Second Crack Black Burnt tones Thin Almost none Low
